Our Mission

Develop robust and resilient artificial intelligence (AI) models to detect, withstand, and mitigate emerging AI-driven cyber threats.

The TrustSec AI Lab advances research at the intersection of AI and cybersecurity, building AI-driven solutions that enhance the security, robustness, and trustworthiness of interconnected digital systems.

Why It Matters: AI powers decision-making across sectors by processing vast, high-velocity data streams. The TrustSec AI Lab ensures these systems remain reliable, robust, and worthy of trust, safeguarding societal impact and mission-critical operations.

Foundational Research Areas

We advance core research in machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) to identify vulnerabilities and build defenses against adversarial AI systems:

  • Network Security — Threat detection across complex, distributed environments
  • Data Leakage — Preventing sensitive information exposure and model inversion
  • Adversarial AI — Attacks, robustness analysis, and certified defenses
  • Large Language Models (LLMs) — Risks, attacks, and secure deployment

Research Focus Areas

Adversarial Robustness

Design and evaluate defense mechanisms to protect AI systems from adversarial attacks.

IoT Privacy in Health

Enhance privacy in health-centric IoT ecosystems using generative models for anonymization.

LLM Hallucination & Privacy

Verify retrieval augmented generation pipelines and apply secure multi-party computation.

Collaboration & Opportunities

✅ UAlbany Research Labs

We welcome collaborations to:

  • Employ DL and transfer learning for data curation and multi-source model training
  • Strengthen privacy in health IoT via federated learning and differential privacy
  • Apply behavioral analytics to detect cyber and physical threats
  • Build anomaly-based detection systems to support red teams

🏛️ Government Agencies

Apply ML to assess AI systems susceptibility to phishing attacks, especially during environmental and societal crises.

👨‍🎓 Students

The lab is open to undergraduate and graduate students with Python experience interested in trustworthy AI and security research.

📚 Educational Impact

  • Teach defense strategies against adversarial ML attacks
  • Assess vulnerabilities in AI models used in CEHC research
  • Equip students with DL skills to design and deploy robust AI
  • Conduct behavioral analytics with industry partners
  • Foster collaborations with cybersecurity and AI industry
